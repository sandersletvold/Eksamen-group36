Fake News and Critical Thinking

One of the most unintended consequences artificial intelligence can bring is that it can potentially lead to fewer jobs. As we know, machines can perform drastically faster work than a human. This results in unemployment rising and the economy slowing. People with low education are going to be most affected. We can also imagine that AI (artificial intelligence), makes processes easier done and accurate analyses. But can also come with unpredictable outcomes that affect individuals, communities or society as a whole.

In addition, the legal justice system must keep a constant eye on how developments will keep pace with the technological laws we have and what their progress will look like in the future. So that it can protect the rights of everyone. The only way to solve this is to develop a legal framework that makes sure that everything complies with the law and create safe regulations that are suitable, so that unique problems are created by artificial intelligence/technology are prevented.

Artificial intelligence can also help spread a lot of misinformation, such as deepfakes, manipulated videos and, for example, the general election we will have in 2025. Stanford University issued an ustenchment, as the following states "Technology can be taken over by criminals, rogue states, ideological extremists or simply special interest groups, in order to manipulate people for financial gain or political advantage", which is a good argument for how dangerous it can be, if the technology falls into the wrong hands. But with proper regulation and a framework, this can be prevented. If Parliament and the Court come together with a proposal that avoids these problems, then this can be adopted.

AI in politics

AI kan ha i politiske påvirkninger i situasjoner og valg. Det er relatert til samfunnet og teknologi fordi politiske kampanjer kan bli påvirket av denne teknologien, noe som går utover samfunnets oppfatninger av for eksempel regjeringen og staten. Et eksempel på dette er i Canada hvor en ordførerkandidat brukte AI-genererte bilder av hjemløse i den byen, hvor han snakket mye om å bekjempe fattigdom og hjemløshet (Hsu & Lee Myers, 2023). Dette kan være problematisk da velgere kan føle seg lurt av at politikere ikke bruker bilder av ekte situasjoner og hendelser.
